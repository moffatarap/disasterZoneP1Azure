Disaster Zone

About

Concept Details

Disaster Zone is a single-page responsive website that brings together information on disasters occurring in the world in real-time to one easy to access website and mobile application. Disaster Zone must scale to all modern devices including desktops, laptops, tablets and mobile devices. Both the website and the mobile application run in tandem, displaying the same info at all times.

Users of the website or the mobile application will be notified of disasters near them when required after a sign-up process. Disaster Zone uses GPS location data along with postcodes and city addresses for the alerts process, if a disaster event is near a user.

The user is informed by SMS, Email or in-app push notification telling them of event and what to do. Users can post warnings about events in their area via the app or website these event notifications can be seen by other users and staff of Disaster Zone in the Google maps API.

All events are quality controlled to make sure they are valid and that the data set is correct and up to date. Disaster Zone acts as an information site to help people in the event of a disaster and to coordinate volunteers in the aftermath of a natural disaster. The other function of is the Disaster Zone relief fund; user-donated money to support people in a disaster zone.

No profit is taken from donations, all funds go directly to where its’ needed. The website is made for all ages; it is designed with simplicity and user ability in mind. Someone with very little technical skills needs to be able to use and operate the website easily and effectively in a time of stress.

Disaster Zone is a concept by Arapaoa Moffat started in project two of MDDN201 2015, and is going to be carried on in development though the duration of MDDN352 2016.

Disaster Zone Severity Scale

  1. Severe level of damage to people and or property, high risk of injury and human casualties. 10,000+ people missing or injured 70% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
  2. Strong level of damage to people and or property, medium high risk of injury and human casualties. 5,000 to 8,000 people missing or injured 60% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
  3. Moderate level of damage to people and or property, medium risk of injury and human casualties. 500 to 1,000 people missing or injured 30% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
  1. Light level of damage to people and or property, low risk of injury and human casualties. 0 to 100 people missing or injured 0% to 15% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.
  2. Weak to no damage to people and or property, low to risk of injury and human casualties. 0 people missing or injured 0% to 5% damage to personal property and city infrastructure.

Reporting Systems

Alert Types

Details of Disaster Zone’s alert system can be found below; all these systems must work interchangeably. Users are alerted though any of these methods below in the event of a disaster near their GPS or given location.

  • -SMS

Notifications sent via phone-tree an online application system to send SMS to users personal mobile devices from the sign-up process.

  • - EMAIL

Notifications sent via Gmail to users personal email address from the sign-up process

  • - PUSH NOTIFICAION

If application is installed push notification on user’s device is displayed.

Reporting

Disaster Zones disaster reporting is done via Google API in website and mobile application. On the map shows users location, and what type and severity it is, along with ability to volunteer or donate if over a defined threshold on the Disaster Zone Severity Scale.

Data for disasters are gathered via official weather and disaster API’s and then added to map in real-time. Users can add notes and their own markers on map from events in a Disaster Zone. All of these requests are quality checked to make sure that no one is misusing the system.

API's

Due to the nature of the website being real-time, API’s are used to gather information and automatically display on the google maps API creating markers. The API’s I’m planning to use can be found below for each disaster type, some are global whereas others are localized to New Zealand.

  1. Earthquakes Geonet API [NZ]
  2. Flood Iris API [NZ]
  3. Hurricane Terrapin API [PACIFIC]
  4. Tornado Aeris API [World Wide]
  5. Fire Nwcg API [US]

Desired Outcome

For the scope of the project I wish to be able to make the map update with real-time information with at least one API, have a working GPS and location based system on both mobile and web. Web design that allows for responsive scaling on all devices creation of email based warnings for disasters.

This will require a lot of code and time to get external weather API’s to function in real-time. As well as a change the website language from html to asp.net hosted online using Microsoft Azure. Disaster Zone is not just a website that is just for reporting events, but as a community and a place for people to get help and support when in a time of great stress and peril.
